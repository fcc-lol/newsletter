Issue 001  ·  November 14, 2025
Welcome to the first issue of the FCC Studio newsletter! We're so excited to have this channel to share our work, inspiration, musings, and happenings at the studio.
The inaugural FCC Studio gallery show
Thank you to everyone who came out to see our show and celebrate at our opening reception! This was a step into the unknown for us and your support meant the world to each of us. Huge thanks to our host, Studio Anselm Dastner, for making it all possible!

If you missed the show, you can watch the entire 28-hour recording on YouTube, including when we set up on Friday. Let us know when you're done. Or, check out our 1 minute walkthrough if you're more pressed for time.
Watching people's live, honest reactions to work we had in some cases barely shown to the outside world was really heartening and enlightening. Two guests who saw Wallflower reflected on AI art, and described their feeling that the AI generated videos truly could not have been easily done by a human using traditional animation techniques.

People seemed initially drawn to the Friendship Beacon for its bold colors and intricacy, but then they stayed and puzzled over it just to try to figure it out. As one of the early projects that inspired us to form the studio, the Friendship Beacon felt like a natural piece to include and we were delighted to see people enjoy it.

Particularly humbling was the number of children who came with their parents to experience the art together. Our youngest visitors brought some of the most creative reactions to the visualizers on the Play Machine Mini, imagining swirling cities of people, surrounded by lightning.

Another highlight for us was how many people couldn't put down the Handheld Computer Vision Rig. The way it "interprets" the parts of the world people pointed it at seemed at times sophisticated, but just as often charmingly naive or hilariously abrupt.
Next up? Rest, slate-cleaning, and then on to new ideas!
We spent the last few weeks planning and preparing for this show, culminating in between 6 hours (Leo's figure) and 9 hours (Dan's definitely correct figure) of setup, and a full weekend of greeting and enjoying our guests. So we're looking forward to recovering a little bit, taking a breath, and regrouping.

We're making space in the studió to help focus on new ideas. So if you or someone you know is interested in purchasing a piece you saw at the show, please reach out.

We have a few project ideas that we're excited about starting soon, including a digital mirror with a camera for generating AI poetry, a physical countdown clock of new rocket launches inside of a decommissioned airplane meal container, and a fine-tuned AI model that can assist in making Traditional Chinese Medicine recommendations.

More to come in future newsletters! As always, please feel free to email us at studio@fcc.lol with ideas, questions, or thoughts!

Be well,
Zach, Dan, and Leo
