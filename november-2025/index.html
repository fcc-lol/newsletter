Welcome to the first issue of the FCC Studio Newsletter! We're excited to share projects, inspiration, and happenings at the studio.
We had our first gallery show over the weekend!
Thanks to everyone who came out to the show and opening reception. It was so meaningful to see so many of you there and we're really grateful for your support. Huge thanks to our host, Studio Anselm Dastner, for making it all possible!

If you missed the show, you can watch the entire 28 hour recording on YouTube, including our 6 hour setup on Friday. Let us know when you're done. There's also this 1 minute walkthrough if you have less time.
It was cool to hear people's reactions, including conversations about AI art. When two people saw Wallflower, they reflected on how it felt like a more interesting use of AI image generation since it was incorporating video that would be hard for a human to create manually.

The Friendship Beacon attracted people with its bold colors and intricate combination of components, which was great to see, especially since this was the project that catalyzed the formation of FCC Studio!

We had a few children come in with their parents and it was great to see how both younger and older people were able to experience the art together. Watching how kids came up with stories in real time while playing with visualizers on the Play Machine Mini was really delightful.

People seemed to have a lot of fun playing with the Handheld Computer Vision Rig. It was awesome to watch how people were using it with their friends and enjoying seeing the world through the lens of AI.
What's next?
First, a little bit of rest. We spent the last few weeks planning and preparing for this show, so we're excited to reset a little bit and take a breath.

We're trying to make space in the studio for new work so if you or someone you know is interested in purchasing a piece from the show, please let us know.

We have a few project ideas that we're excited about starting soon, including a digital mirror with a camera for generating AI poetry, a physical countdown clock of new rocket launches inside of a decommissioned airplane meal container, and a fine-tuned AI model that can assist in making Traditional Chinese Medicine recommendations.

We'll share more in future newsletters! As always, please feel free to email us at studio@fcc.lol with ideas, questions, or thoughts!

Be well,
Zach, Dan, and Leo
